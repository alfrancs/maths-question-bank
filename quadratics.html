Expanding & Factorising

Worked Example

Expand and simplify: (x + 3)(x + 5)
Step 1: Use FOIL or the grid method
"We multiply each term in the first bracket by each term in the second bracket"
First: x √ó x = x¬≤
Outer: x √ó 5 = 5x
Inner: 3 √ó x = 3x
Last: 3 √ó 5 = 15
Step 2: Combine like terms
x¬≤ + 5x + 3x + 15
x¬≤ + 8x + 15
Remind students that factorising is the reverse process - we're looking for two brackets that multiply to give the quadratic!

Solving via Factorisation

Worked Example

Solve: x¬≤ + 5x + 6 = 0
Step 1: Factorize the quadratic
"We're looking for two numbers that multiply to give 6 and add to give 5"
Those numbers are 2 and 3
(x + 2)(x + 3) = 0
Step 2: Apply the null factor law
"If two things multiply to give zero, one of them must be zero"
Either (x + 2) = 0 OR (x + 3) = 0
Step 3: Solve each bracket
x + 2 = 0 ‚Üí x = -2
x + 3 = 0 ‚Üí x = -3
Remind students why we get TWO solutions - parabolas cross the x-axis twice!
